Research Overview

The Agroecosystem Modeling Lab investigates how agricultural management and environmental change interact to shape agroecosystem productivity, resilience, and sustainability. Our research integrates process-based agroecosystem modeling, remote sensing, field observations, and AI-driven methods to understand and predict system responses across spatial and temporal scales. Our work spans both (1) fundamental research on coupled carbon, water, and nutrient processes in agroecosystems under environmental stress, and (2) applied research that translates these insights into decision-support tools and management strategies that enhance productivity while addressing challenges such as greenhouse gas emissions, soil degradation, and water pollution.

Developing Agroecosystem Models


Guiding question: How can agroecosystems be represented to realistically capture interactions among management, environment, and biophysical processes across scales? We develop and advance process-based, data-driven, and hybrid agroecosystem models that explicitly represent crop and grassland growth, soil carbon and nitrogen dynamics, water balance, and greenhouse gas emissions. Our modeling frameworks incorporate agricultural management practices—such as fertilization, irrigation, tillage, cover cropping, and crop–livestock integration—and are designed to operate from field to regional scales.

Understanding Agroecosystem Processes


Guiding question: How do agroecosystem processes respond to environmental change and management? We investigate the mechanisms controlling agroecosystem function by quantifying coupled carbon, water, and nutrient processes and their responses to climate variability, climate change, elevated CO₂, and management practices. This work focuses on identifying key drivers, thresholds, and feedbacks that regulate productivity, resource use efficiency, and ecosystem stability in croplands and grasslands.

Predicting Agroecosystem Responses


Guiding question: How will agroecosystems respond to future climate variability and extremes? We integrate process-based models, remote sensing, and AI-enabled approaches to predict agroecosystem responses to droughts, heatwaves, and other climate extremes under future climate scenarios. This research supports early warning, scenario analysis, and assessment of risk and resilience for agricultural systems under changing environmental conditions.

Designing Agroecosystem Management Practices


Guiding question: Which management strategies can improve productivity and resilience while reducing environmental impacts? We apply systems-level understanding and predictive tools to evaluate and design agroecosystem management practices, including fertilization strategies, irrigation management, tillage systems, cover cropping, crop rotations, and integrated crop–livestock systems. This work identifies tradeoffs and synergies among yield stability, soil health, greenhouse gas emissions, nutrient losses, and water use efficiency to support science-based management and decision-making.
