Research Overview

The Agroecosystem Modeling Lab investigates how agricultural management and environmental change interact to shape agroecosystem productivity, resilience, and sustainability. Our research integrates process-based agroecosystem modeling, remote sensing, field observations, and AI-driven methods to understand and predict system responses across spatial and temporal scales. Our work spans both (1) fundamental research on coupled carbon, water, and nutrient processes in agroecosystems under environmental stress, and (2) applied research that translates these insights into decision-support tools and management strategies that enhance productivity while addressing challenges such as greenhouse gas emissions, soil degradation, and water pollution.

Agroecosystem Model Development


We develop agroecosystem models that represent crop and grassland growth, soil carbon and nitrogen cycling, water balance, and greenhouse gas emissions. Our work includes process-based models, data-driven approaches, and hybrid frameworks that integrate mechanistic understanding with machine learning to capture interactions among management, climate, and biophysical processes across field to regional scales.

Sustainable Agricultural Management Practices


We examine how agricultural management practices (e.g., tillage, cover cropping, irrigation, and nutrient management) influence productivity, yield stability, soil health, and environmental outcomes. This research evaluates management strategies across cropping systems to identify practices that support long-term sustainability and resilience under variable environmental conditions.

Integrated Crop–Livestock Systems


We study integrated crop–livestock systems to understand how linking cropping and grazing affects productivity, resource use efficiency, soil carbon storage, and greenhouse gas emissions. Our work focuses on cropland–livestock interactions, grazing management, and nutrient recycling, with an emphasis on system-level tradeoffs and synergies.

Agroecosystem Responses to Climate Extremes and Environmental Change


We investigate how agroecosystems respond to CO2 fertilization, climate change, and extreme events such as droughts and heatwaves. Using models, remote sensing, and observational data, this research assesses vulnerability, resilience, and recovery of agricultural systems under current and future environmental conditions.
